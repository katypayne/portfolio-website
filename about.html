About Me

My Story

Hi, I’m Katy Payne-Djalé (pronounced like “jolly”) and I’m born and raised in the beautiful green and mountainous landscape of Washington State!

I enjoy spending time outdoors or traveling with my husband Claude and I like to refer to myself as a Jill-of-all-trades. Whether it’s sports, DIY projects, writing music, reading, or anything in-between, I enjoy exploring every avenue of creativity that life has to offer.

I’m bi-vocational as a UX Designer and as a non-denominational pastor (who happens to take her whiskey neat) and I believe that blending these two fields highlights the true features of who I am. I’m an empath at heart and I enjoy understanding the stories and experiences that shape the people around me. I possess a unique ability to be able to socialize and find common ground with someone of any age, background, or personality type.

I believe that when you can understand why someone operates the way that they do, then you can help them grow into the person they want to become. This is my primary role as a pastor, and it just so happens to also be my primary role as a designer, since design work is all about empathizing with what the user needs.

I desire to do purposeful UX Design work that helps the user become the best versions of themselves and that adds value to the world around me. That’s why I’m passionate about projects in the vein of self-help and the non-profit sector.
