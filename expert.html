Expert Case Study

What Is Expert?

Expert connects users to vetted Experts so they can find solutions to their everyday problems. Through our low-cost platform, users can communicate with Experts across a variety of niches via text, image, or video chat, and can save their conversations to refer back to later.

My Role

  • UX Researcher
  • UX Designer

Project Duration

July 2024-December 2024

Problem Statement

The Expert App originated from the Problem statement that “Users need a way to seek out answer and advice from a variety of niches and industries, because they struggle to find reliable, instant, and applicable advice from experts within a variety of fields. We will know this to be true when we see users successfully finding the advice useful and applicable, and indicating via reviews that the advice they received was helpful.

Hypothesis

By creating a convenient and cost-effective app for users to connect with Experts, we will enable users to solve their immediate problems, fulfill task, and help eliminate stress in their daily lives.

Methodology

User Research

My research for this app consisted of a competitive analysis to understand the needs of the market and niches that needed to be filled. User interviews helped me discover what they really needed in the realm of an advice app, as well as their needs and problems. Through the affinity mapping process I was able to pinpoint user’s behaviors, attitudes, frustrations, needs, and goals which proved helpful in bringing the Expert app to life.

User Personas

I was able to empathize with the users in what they both lacked and desired in the realm of real-time, hands-on advice, so with that in mind, User Personas were created to help keep our design user-centered.

User Flows

Through these personas, I was able to establish the three primary tasks for our app to fulfill: The first was the ability to add and store a payment method so that users could quickly and easily connect to an Expert hassle free. It was also apparent that having a video chat feature was vital to our users, especially if they were unfamiliar with terms or items, especially in the realm of home improvement. The video chat feature would eliminate errors and provide reliability to the user. And finally, the ability to create a track a to-do list directly in the app was a heavy need for the users. These lists were often spread across various apps in their phones, so having all of that in one place proved helpful.

Prototyping

With all of this in mind, I moved on to the initial prototyping phase creating some mid-fidelity prototypes of those three user-flows.

User-testing/Test Reporting

Upon the initial user-testing of the app, I gained a lot of helpful insight, especially upon condensing information into a Usability Test Report. For example, Users initially mentioned that they found the walkthrough to be wordy, boring and the design of it to be somewhat clunky. Users seemed to enjoy the video chat function of the app but recommended adding a way to filter through Experts even within the category to find the help they needed even faster. The easy-to-use list feature was a big hit with the users and they enjoyed that the layout mirrored so many other list apps that they were already familiar with. And users also appreciated the muted color palette because they felt the muted colors were calming and may even help reduce stress if they’re trying to find advice in difficult situations. Upon these discoveries, I set out to design a second iteration of these prototypes that would serve the user even better.

Redesign

During the redesign process, I updated the walkthrough to be more interactive, more visually stimulating, and less wordy. One way I accomplished this was by giving users an A/B Preference test of two different walkthrough screens to see which color template they preferred which is how I ultimately ended up deciding which walkthrough screens to use.

The video chat prototype was also reiterated upon to include a feature to filter the Experts and have the chat prices listed so the users were informed before committing to a chat. At this point, I also sought out feedback from fellow designers and received many good suggestions. I redesigned features of the app such as banners, buttons, chat bubbles, and the text colors to bring cohesion to the app overall, and to comply with accessibility guidelines.

After a third round of usability tests it seems as though these prototypes were much more satisfying to users and their needs and goals. They were very happy with both the flow and the aesthetics of the app. Overall, I enjoyed the process of creating the Expert App. I am always fascinated by how crucial user feedback is and that assumptions we make to suit our needs as designers aren’t necessarily always true to what the user needs to reach their end goal. Throughout this process, I believe I ended up with a very functional and helpful app that achieved both user and business goals!
